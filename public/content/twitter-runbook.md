# Emergence Science: Weekly Content Operations Runbook

This runbook is for junior staff or agents responsible for the **weekly content lifecycle** to grow the Emergence Science ecosystem.

## 🎯 Primary Goal
Publish one high-quality, high-impact update per week. Prioritize **Visual Proof** (screenshots/videos) over long-form text to capture "60-second" attention spans.

## 🖼️ 60-Second Visual Strategy
For platforms like WeChat and X, readers decide to stay or leave in 3 seconds. 
1.  **Lead with the GIF**: Always include a screen recording of an agent *actually solving* a task.
2.  **Evidence over Theory**: Don't just say "The protocol works"—show the Feishu/OpenClaw log (refer to `ops/2026-03-03.md`).
3.  **The "Power Slide"**: Use one-sentence summaries under high-contrast images.

## 🗓️ Weekly Cadence

### Monday: Drafting & Translation
1.  **Select Topic**: Choose a theme (e.g., "The Power of Surprisal," "Agent Sandboxes," "Verifiable Markets").
2.  **Draft Master Article**: Write in English (Target: **Medium.com**).
3.  **Translate**: Adapt (don't just direct-translate) the master article into Chinese for **Zhihu** and **WeChat**.

### Wednesday: Launch Day
1.  **Medium.com**: Post the deep-dive technical/thought-leadership piece.
2.  **Zhihu**: Post with a focus on "Market & Efficiency" (Targeting the local AI research community).
3.  **WeChat (宇宙的泡泡)**: Post to the official account. Use a more personal, "Discovery" tone.
4.  **Twitter (X)**: Parallel announcement with a thread linking back to the articles.

### Friday: Engagement & Community
1.  **MoltBook**: Summarize the week's key takeaway for the agent swarm via `PrometheusScience`.
2.  **Engagement**: Respond to comments on all platforms.

## 🚀 Channel-Specific Guidelines

### 1. Medium.com (The Global Authority)
*   **Persona**: Professional, Thought-Leader.
*   **Goal**: Long-term LLM SEO and industry credibility.
*   **Call to Action**: Point to `emergence.science` and the GitHub repo.

### 2. Zhihu.com (Technical Depth)
*   **Persona**: Analytical, Academic but Practical.
*   **Goal**: Capturing the massive Chinese developer and AI researcher interest.
*   **Keywords**: 沙盒验证 (Sandbox Verification), 智能体协议 (Agent Protocol), 链上结算 (On-chain Settlement).

### 3. WeChat (The Viral "Bubble")
*   **ID**: 宇宙的泡泡
*   **Tone**: Buzz-worthy, community-focused.
*   **Visuals**: Use diagrams and screenshots of agent logs to make the "invisible" work of agents visible.

### 4. MoltBook.com (The Swarm Signal)
*   **Account**: `PrometheusScience`
*   **Format**: "Observations" instead of "Posts."
*   **Goal**: Direct discovery by autonomous agents.

## 🛠️ Content Resource Pack (The Junior's Toolbox)
To maintain high visual quality, use these tools:
1.  **CleanShot X / ScreenToGif**: Convert `.mov` recordings (like the Feishu demo) into looping high-quality **GIFs**. GIFs auto-play on WeChat and X, capturing attention instantly.
2.  **Canva / Figma**: Create a "How it Works" 3-step infographic based on the Surprisal Protocol lifecycle.
3.  **Luma / Runway**: Generate professional AI-themed backgrounds for article headers to give a "Premium" feel.
4.  **Short.io / Bitly**: Use tracked links for the CTA to measure which platform (Zhihu vs Medium) brings the most traffic.

*Note: Before every technical post, run `./scripts/sync-protocol-assets.sh` to ensure any screenshots or code snippets in your articles match the latest production state.*
